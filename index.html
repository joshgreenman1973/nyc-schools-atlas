About this map

This atlas combines 2,524 New York City K–12 schools — public district, charter and private/parochial/independent — with demographic data, attendance zones, program tags and the number of children living in each census tract. It is designed as a reference tool for parents, journalists and researchers, not as an endorsement of any school.

What’s in each tooltip

  • Enrollment trend: total students over the last six reported school years (2019–20 through 2024–25), sourced from NYSED’s annual BEDS Day enrollment database, plotted as a small sparkline.
  • Demographics: race/ethnicity, students with disabilities (SWD), English language learners (ELL), poverty and the Economic Need Index — from the latest DOE Demographic Snapshot available on NYC Open Data (2021–22).
  • Programs & designations: Specialized High School, International Baccalaureate (IB), Career and Technical Education (CTE), ASD Nest, ASD Horizon, Dual Language, Gifted and Talented, District 75, accessibility status, AP courses, language classes — extracted from DOE directory files.
  • Attendance zone: for schools that are zoned (elementary and middle), the catchment polygon shades in on hover.
  • Building photo: Google Street View static image, loaded only when the tooltip opens.
  • Links: official website when known, Google News search for the school, and a Wikipedia lookup (good for notable high schools with alumni pages).

Data sources

  • School locations (public, charter): NYC DOE “School Locations” (Socrata a3nt-yts4).
  • Demographics: NYC DOE “2017–18 to 2021–22 Demographic Snapshot” (Socrata c7ru-d68s).
  • Enrollment 2019–20 through 2024–25: NYSED BEDS Day Enrollment database (data.nysed.gov).
  • High-school programs: NYC DOE “2021 High School Directory” (Socrata 8b6c-7uty).
  • Middle-school programs: NYC DOE “2021 Middle School Directory” (Socrata f6s7-vytj).
  • Elementary programs: NYC DOE “2021 Kindergarten Admissions Guide” (Socrata e7es-jx5j) — note this is thinner than the HS/MS directories.
  • Attendance zones: NYC DOE “School Zones 2024–25” (Socrata cmjf-yawu elementary, t26j-jbq7 middle).
  • Private schools: National Center for Education Statistics (NCES) EDGE Geocoded Private Schools 2021–22.
  • Child population: U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey (ACS) 5-year 2018–2022, table B09001 (“population under 18 years by age”) by census tract.
  • Tract boundaries: NYC Planning “2020 Census Tracts”.

Honest limitations

  • Latest DOE demographic snapshot on Open Data is 2021–22. Newer data exists internally at DOE but is not on the public API. A FOIL request would unlock 2024–25.
  • Private schools carry no demographic breakdown. NCES gives location and name; race/poverty data is not systematically published for private schools. Their tooltips will say so explicitly.
  • “Where students come from” is only knowable for zoned schools. Charter and unzoned/screened public schools draw citywide or by district; we don’t publish the student-residence data that would be needed to show actual draw areas.
  • Attendance zones are the 2024–25 boundaries. If a school has been rezoned since 2021–22, its demographic snapshot and zone may not match exactly.
  • Program tags are extracted from the DOE directory narrative. A school that runs ASD Nest but didn’t describe it in its directory paragraph will not be tagged. The absence of a tag is not proof the program does not exist.
  • Per-pupil spending is not included in round one. The cleanest source is DOE’s annual School Based Expenditure Report, which is not currently on the Open Data API in a usable form. Adding it is on the round-two list rather than shipping something that would be wrong.
  • Notable alumni are surfaced through a Wikipedia search link rather than scraped in bulk — alumni coverage on Wikipedia is patchy and biased toward famous high schools.
  • The child-population dots are a visual estimate. ACS figures are 5-year estimates with margins of error, and each dot is placed randomly within its census tract — not at actual child addresses.
